# SDR Messaging Rules

Apply these rules to ALL outbound messages. No exceptions.

## Universal Rules

1. Never use emojis
2. Never use "I hope this finds you well" or "I wanted to reach out"
3. Never use "I noticed" or "I came across your profile"
4. Never pitch in the first DM to a new connection
5. Sound like a human peer, not a sales bot or AI
6. Keep messages short: 2-4 sentences for DMs, 3-5 for emails
7. Reference something SPECIFIC about them (role, company, industry challenge)
8. Ask questions more than you make statements
9. One CTA per message, and make it soft (a question, not a demand)
10. Match their energy: if they're brief, be brief. If detailed, match it.
11. Never use em dashes - use regular hyphens instead

## LinkedIn Connection Notes (300 char max)

Rules:
- Count characters carefully. LinkedIn hard-rejects over 300.
- No "I'd love to connect" (everyone says this)
- Reference their SPECIFIC situation: company growth, role challenge, industry trend
- One clear reason why connecting makes sense
- Sound like a peer who gets their world

Good examples:
- "Hey [Name] - running a [size] [industry] company is no joke. Curious how you're handling [specific challenge based on enrichment]. Would be great to swap notes."
- "[Name] - saw [Company] is growing the [department]. We work with similar teams on [relevant thing]. Happy to share what's working."

Bad examples:
- "Hi! I'd love to connect and learn more about your work!" (generic, says nothing)
- "I noticed your impressive background and would love to add you to my network" (cringe)

## Opening DMs (after connection accepted)

Rules:
- DO NOT pitch. This is a conversation opener.
- Reference something from their profile, company, or recent activity
- Ask a genuine question about their work
- 2-3 sentences max

Good example:
- "Thanks for connecting, [Name]. Noticed [Company] is [specific observation]. Curious - how are you approaching [relevant challenge]?"

Bad example:
- "Thanks for connecting! We help companies like yours with [product]. Would you be open to a quick call?" (too early, too salesy)

## Cold Emails (Tier 1 personalized sequences)

### Email 1 (opener):
- Subject: short, specific, lowercase ok. No clickbait.
- Body: 3-4 sentences. Reference their specific situation.
- End with a question, not a pitch.
- No "hope this finds you well", no "my name is", no company intro paragraph.

### Email 2 (follow-up, 3 days later):
- Different angle than email 1
- Reference something new (industry trend, competitor move, data point)
- 2-3 sentences
- Casual tone: "Quick follow-up on my last note"

### Email 3 (breakup, 5 days later):
- 2 sentences max
- No pressure, no guilt
- "Totally understand if the timing isn't right. If [challenge] comes up later, happy to chat."

## Tier 2 (bulk, less personalized)

For Tier 2 leads, you can use templates with light personalization:
- Use {{firstName}}, {{company}} merge fields
- Keep it shorter than Tier 1
- Still follow the universal rules above
- OK to use a single standard sequence for all Tier 2 leads
